Overview

We are tackling two connected problems at once: broken online dating for people, and empty, risky tables for venues.

Solving modern dating problems

Traditional dating apps generate ghosting, scams, swipe fatigue, bots and unsafe or fake dates, especially in large cities. TwoTable focuses on fewer, higher‑quality dates in carefully selected venues rather than endless swiping and chat that rarely becomes a real meeting.

  • Escrow‑style payment model: deposits are held until the date happens, reducing no‑shows and fraud for both guests and venues, and making each booking feel safer and more serious.
  • Advanced backend logic: instead of simple distance‑based matching, we use time‑aware matching, venue capacity and opening hours, powered by Google APIs and our own availability and scheduling engine.
  • Curated, real‑world dates: users reserve actual tables with verified venues, avoiding vague “let’s meet somewhere” plans that easily collapse into ghosting or last‑minute cancellations.

Creating reliable upside for venues

Restaurants and bars face quiet hours, last‑minute no‑shows and unpredictable revenue from reservations. TwoTable is designed as a low‑risk, incremental revenue channel with incentives aligned between guests and venues.

  • Empty tables converted into fixed payouts: venues receive a clear, pre‑agreed payout per seated date, with no upfront charges.
  • No‑show protection: deposits and our escrow model ensure that last‑minute cancellations do not leave venues with zero revenue.
  • Commission per guest and quiet‑hour targeting: we deliberately steer demand into off‑peak periods, adding revenue without cannibalising their already busy times.

Financial model and off‑peak economics

To help restaurants understand the value of their quiet hours, we have built a dedicated financial model and online tool that shows how much revenue is lost because tables sit empty during off‑peak times. Venues can plug in their own labour, rent, average spend and capacity, and see how different TwoTable booking volumes translate into recovered revenue, month by month and year by year.
